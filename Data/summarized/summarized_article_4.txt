"Kansas City Shooting Raises Concerns Over Homicide Rates and Gun Laws in Missouri"
One person died and 22 were injured in a shooting at a Kansas City Chiefs' Super Bowl parade. The incident highlights the city's record-breaking homicide rate and lax gun laws in Missouri. Two juveniles were detained in connection with the shooting, with no charges filed yet. The mayor emphasized the difference between daily crime and the public attack. Missouri's gun laws are among the weakest in the US, with no background checks required for gun purchases. Missouri's House rejected a ban on minors openly carrying firearms without adult supervision.
